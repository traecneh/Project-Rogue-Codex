Corruption System

How corruption replaces innate perks, where corrupted items drop, and how to cleanse them.

Corruption Roles

Override

Corrupted Innate

Corruption replaces the item's normal innate perk line with its corrupted perk line.

Sources

Hard Bosses

Corrupted loot is tied to hard boss and corrupted-monster sources; item pages are the place to confirm exact drops.

Cleanse

Cleanse with Purge

Use the Purge Tool when the goal is removing a corrupted innate.

Plan

Check Gear Pages

Weapon and armor detail panels show both normal perks and Corrupted Perk values when the data has them.

What Corruption Changes

Corrupted Item

Innate Line Changes

  • The item keeps its identity, slot, rarity state, base requirements, and normal stat ranges.
  • The normal innate perk is overridden by the item's corrupted perk.
  • Some items use a specific alternate perk rather than a simple tier increase.

Cleaned Item

Normal Innate Returns

  • Cleanse targets the corrupted innate, not an applied imbuement or special effect.
  • After cleansing, evaluate the item by its normal innate and remaining gear values.
  • Use Purge for cleanup; use Re-Roll or Ascend for item improvement decisions.

Cleanse Flow

  1. 1

    Inspect the item

    Confirm the unwanted line is the Corrupted Perk, not a crafted imbuement or normal innate.

  2. 2

    Compare the perk value

    Check whether the corrupted perk helps the build before removing it.

  3. 3

    Use Purge Tool cleanse

    Cleanse through Purge when you want the item back on its normal innate track.

  4. 4

    Rebuild around the result

    After cleansing, revisit perks, imbuements, rarity, and re-roll decisions as separate systems.

No Item Reset

Before You Cleanse

Cleanse is a targeted cleanup action, not a full item reset. It does not reroll rarity, restore a removed imbuement, or change the item's other values, so only remove corruption when the normal innate is better for the build.

Anti-Zerg System

Dynamic PvP scaling that cushions smaller squads while keeping large zergs honest.

Why Anti-Zerg Exists

Anti-Zerg is a safety net for outnumbered teams in PvP. Whenever your squad is smaller than the force engaging you, the system trims incoming damage so you can trade blows without being erased by raw headcount. The same logic also dials back self-healing for anyone who is currently protected, preventing bunker-style builds from stacking invulnerability and sustain.

How Zerg Size Is Determined

Anti-Zerg works by comparing two Zerg sizes: yours and your attacker’s. The game constantly evaluates your guildmates through two complementary modes and a short-term memory, described below.

Warfront Mode

  • A Warfront bundles several marked PvP areas into a single Anti-Zerg zone.
  • Your minimap explicitly shows when you’re inside a Warfront.
  • While you stay inside, the system counts every guildmate who is also within that Warfront. That number becomes your current Zerg size for PvP calculations.
  • Stepping out of the Warfront boundary immediately swaps you back to Range-Based Mode.

Range-Based Mode

  • Used whenever you are not flagged as being inside a Warfront.
  • You automatically form a Zerg with any guildmate located within 10 map chunks (160 tiles) of your current position.
  • All qualifying guildmates within that circular radius are lumped together for your Zerg size.

Zerg Status Memory (180 Seconds)

  • Meeting the requirements for either Warfront Mode or Range-Based Mode marks you as sharing a Zerg with those guildmates for the next 180 seconds (3 minutes).
  • During that window, temporary spacing or repositioning does not immediately dissolve the Zerg; the system still treats you as linked until the timer expires.

Damage Reduction & Self-Healing

Once your Zerg size is known, Anti-Zerg compares it directly against the attacker’s Zerg size and pulls a value from the internal damage reduction chart (not shown here). Only targets of PvP damage receive the reduction, ensuring outnumbered players survive longer when focus-fired by a swarm.

Any time you benefit from that reduction, the game applies the same percentage as a penalty to your offensive self-healing (heals triggered by attacking others). This blocks double-dipping: you can either be tankier or burst-heal at full value, but not both simultaneously under Anti-Zerg protection.

Anti-Zerg System

PvP scaling that protects smaller squads and mirrors that protection as an offensive self-heal penalty.

Anti-Zerg at a Glance

Purpose

Smaller Squad Cushion

Targets of PvP damage receive reduction when their guild size is smaller than the attacker's side.

Modes

Warfront & Open-World

The system runs in Warfront zones and open-world range/hash checks.

Penalty

Self-Heal Mirror

The same percentage is applied as a penalty to offensive self-healing against protected targets.

Cap

30% Maximum

Published reduction ranges cap at 30% for the smaller qualifying squad.

Mode and Sizing Rules

Open-World Mode

3-Minute High-Water

  • Zerg size is the highest guild count in your area over the last 3 minutes.
  • The world is split into 128x128 Tiles hashes, or 8x8 chunks.
  • Range-based sizing groups guildmates within 10 chunks (160 tiles).
  • Guildmates in your hash or immediately surrounding hashes count toward your Zerg.
  • Underground hashes are calculated with the hashes directly above them.

Warfront Mode

Bundled Zones

  • A Warfront bundles marked PvP areas into one Anti-Zerg zone.
  • While inside, every guildmate in the Warfront counts toward your Zerg size.
  • Leaving the Warfront boundary switches sizing back to range-based mode.
  • Qualifying links persist for 180 seconds, even if spacing changes.

Focus Fire and Collaboration

  1. 1

    Become active

    After 10+ direct attacks in 10 minutes, your guild is an active PvP participant.

  2. 2

    Pick a focus target

    If over 66% of your guild's damage hits one guild, that guild becomes your focus target.

  3. 3

    Combine collaborators

    Multiple guilds focusing the same target are collaborators and their Anti-Zerg values combine.

  4. 4

    Validate effective hits

    Collaboration uses a 5-Minute Activity window, a 10.0 EFFECTIVE attack threshold per Zerg member, and weapon-speed scaling from 750 to 1500.

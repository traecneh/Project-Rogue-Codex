Monster Damage Reduction

Current in-game scaling for high-level monsters: starts at +20, caps at +30, and tops out at a 25% shift.

Monster Damage Reduction at a Glance

Start Threshold

+20 level gap

No monster scaling applies until the monster is more than 20 levels above you.

Full Effect

+30 level gap

At +30 or higher, the monster scaling is fully capped.

Max Shift

25%

Monsters can deal up to 25% more damage and take up to 25% less damage.

Level 100 Cap

Monsters 100+

For this calculation, monsters 100+ count as 100 before the level gap is checked.

Scaling Rules

Below Start

0%

Gaps at +20 or lower have no damage shift.

Ramp

Linear +20 to +30

Each level inside the +20 to +30 window increases the shift toward the 25% cap.

Capped

+30 and above

The same thresholds apply at every player level, and gaps above +30 stay capped.

Threshold Reference

+20 or less

0%

No monster bonus and no reduction against your damage.

+25

12.5%

Monster damage dealt is 112.5%; your damage vs monster is 87.5%.

+30 or more

25%

Monster damage dealt is 125%; your damage vs monster is 75%.

Level 100 Cap

100

Monsters above level 100 use treated level 100 for this calculation.

Example Outcomes

Even or Lower

No scaling

A monster at or below your level does not trigger this system.

Player 25 vs Monster 50

12.5% shift

A +25 gap is halfway through the ramp, so monster damage is 112.5% and your damage is 87.5%.

Player 25 vs Monster 120

25% cap

Monster 120 is treated as 100, producing a capped result of 125% monster damage and 75% player damage.

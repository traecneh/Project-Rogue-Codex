Ascend System

Spend Ascendency Shards to push keeper gear upward until it reaches its item ceiling.

What Ascend Uses

Currency

Ascendency Shards

Ascend spends the shards generated by deconstructing unwanted gear.

Item Ceiling

Max Rarity

Every item has a maximum rarity tier. Capped items cannot be promoted further.

Upgrade Step

One Tier

Each successful ascend raises the item's current rarity by one tier.

Cost Preview

Promotion Cost

Weapon and armor detail pages expose each item's promotion value.

Menu

Backpack Items

The Ascend menu lists eligible backpack items with costs, stat totals, and current rolls.

What Changes

Changes

  • Current Rarity moves up one tier per promotion.
  • Rarity-based stat ranges and item power follow the new tier.
  • Armor bonuses and perk eligibility follow the Rarity ladder.

What Stays Fixed

  • Item Identity stays the same weapon or armor.
  • Max Rarity remains the item ceiling.
  • Drop source, craft source, base values, and item detail data stay tied to the item.

Ascend or Save

Ascend

  • The item has a strong base, useful source, or a roll you want to keep improving.
  • The item can still climb toward a meaningful Max Rarity.
  • The Promotion Cost is worth spending before upgrading another item.

Save Shards

  • The item is close to its ceiling but still not worth building around.
  • The shard cost delays promoting a better weapon or armor piece.
  • The item should be deconstructed or re-rolled before more shards go into it.

Ascend Flow

  1. 1

    Pick a keeper

    Start with gear whose base value, source, or current roll makes it worth promoting.

  2. 2

    Check the ceiling

    Confirm the item's Max Rarity so you know how far it can climb.

  3. 3

    Review the cost

    Compare the Promotion Cost against your available Ascendency Shards.

  4. 4

    Promote one tier

    Select the item in the Ascend menu and confirm when the shard spend is worth it.

Before Spending Shards

Ascending is best treated as a keeper-item decision. Check the item page first for Max Rarity, Deconstruction value, and Promotion Cost; then use the Rarity page to judge what the next tier adds.

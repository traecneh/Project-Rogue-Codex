Project Rogue Codex

Project Rogue Timeline

Project Rogue didn’t appear overnight. It is the latest chapter in a decades-long experiment with Ultima-inspired sandbox MMOs, Dransik spinoffs, and community-built shards. Scroll the highlights below to see how the scene evolved into today’s hardcore PvP sandbox.

1998 – 1999

Dransik Classic

The original Ultima-style 2D MMO launches and sparks the community that will eventually chase every successor.

2002 – 2003

Ashen Empires Era

Upgraded Dransik enters open beta in September 2002 and rebrands as Ashen Empires when the pay-to-play servers open in March 2003.

2004 – 2006

Classic Goes Dark & Returns

Dransik Classic shuts down, then resurfaces in April 2006 under Iron Will / Pixel Mine, keeping the nostalgia flame alive.

2005

Lothgar Online v1

Jason Ely leaves the Dransik team and ships Lothgar Online as a spiritual clone, proving there is room for parallel projects.

Mid-2000s

Vorlia Online

Fans spin up Vorlia Online, a grassroots Dransik-like MMO that later serves as the foundation for Project Rogue.

2013

Elderlands Alpha

Ely’s new 2D engine enters open alpha, fusing retro combat with modern tech and inspiring more experiments in the subgenre.

2016

Lothgar Reboot Plans

While leading Asylumsoft on Elderlands, Ely announces a return of Lothgar Online styled after classic Dransik.

Mar 20 2017

Lothgar Relaunch Falters

The relaunch arrives with hype but fizzles quickly—players cite poor direction and dev bias, prompting the hunt for a better home.

Jul 2017

Project Rogue Begins

Arakard and Acry reboot Vorlia as Project Rogue: 100-level cap, revamped stat math, proven Dransik-era systems, and a PvP logout timer to curb exploits.

2017 – Today

Fresh Wipes & Live Upkeep

Regular wipes, balance passes, and surprise content drops keep the server packed- every fresh start feels like a festival.
